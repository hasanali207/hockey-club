What are the basic rules of hockey?

Hockey involves two teams competing to score goals by hitting a puck into the opponent's net. Rules include offside, icing, and penalties for various infractions.

What equipment do I need to play hockey?

Essential equipment includes a helmet, skates, gloves, stick, and protective gear (shoulder pads, elbow pads, shin guards, and a cup). Goalkeepers require additional specialized gear.

How many players are on a hockey team?

In a standard game, each team has six players on the ice: three forwards, two defensemen, and one goaltender.

What is the difference between field hockey and ice hockey?

While both sports involve hitting a puck or ball with a stick, field hockey is played on grass or artificial turf, and ice hockey is played on an ice rink with skates.

How long does a typical hockey game last?

Professional hockey games typically consist of three periods, each lasting 20 minutes. Overtime and shootouts may be used to determine a winner if the score is tied.

What is a power play in hockey?

A power play occurs when one team has more players on the ice due to the other team's penalties. The team with the advantage aims to capitalize on the opportunity to score.
